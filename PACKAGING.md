## 1.1 Neck / thread finish — GPI 63/2030 (mandatory)

The neck finish is the single most important geometry on the jar — it determines whether **every** cap you ever buy will fit, and whether the induction seal can find a flat, clean rim to bond to. We standardize on **GPI 63/2030**, the wide-mouth food-jar finish used by every major Indian glass house and every cap manufacturer. No bespoke geometry.

> Visual rendering of these dimensions (with engaged thread + foil + cap) lives at **[/label-preview](https://mothersgoldspice.com/label-preview)**, section 5.

### Required dimensions

| Symbol | Meaning | Value | Tolerance |
|--------|---------|-------|-----------|
| **T** | Thread outside diameter (crest of thread, at neck) | **62.4 mm** | ± 0.4 mm |
| **E** | Root diameter (straight neck wall under threads) | **60.2 mm** | ± 0.4 mm |
| **I** | Mouth inside diameter (opening) | **50.0 mm** | ± 0.5 mm |
| **S** | Rim outside diameter (top sealing land) | **66.0 mm** | ± 0.4 mm |
| **H** | Neck height from shoulder to top sealing surface | **12.5 mm** | ± 0.5 mm |
| **Pitch** | Distance one thread climbs per full revolution | **6.0 mm** | ± 0.2 mm |
| **Lead** | Number of thread starts (single-lead) | **1** | — |
| **Thread turns** | From start (just above shoulder) to end (just below rim) | **2.5** | nominal |
| **Thread direction** | Right-hand (standard, opens counter-clockwise from above) | **RH** | — |
| **Sealing land width** | Flat top ring where induction foil bonds | **1.5 mm** | ± 0.2 mm |
| **Sealing land flatness** | Critical for induction-seal adhesion — no waves, no slopes | **≤ 0.1 mm deviation** | strict |

### Critical surface — the top sealing land

The top of the rim is **the most important 1.5mm of the entire jar**. The induction foil bonds to this ring; if it has bumps, slopes, or a parting-line ridge from the mould, the seal fails and oil leaks. Confirm with the factory:

- The sealing land is **machined or fire-polished flat** (no parting line on the sealing surface)
- The parting line of the mould must run **down the body**, never across the rim
- Surface roughness on the sealing land: **Ra ≤ 0.4 µm** (smooth enough for a foil to bond uniformly)
- Edge between rim OD and sealing land: **softly broken (~0.2 mm chamfer)** — no sharp burrs that nick foil

### Cap matching specification (must also be in §2)

The cap manufacturer must produce internal threads that mate with GPI 63/2030:

- **Internal thread major diameter:** 62.8 mm (slightly larger than T = 62.4 to allow clearance)
- **Internal thread root diameter:** 60.0 mm
- **Same pitch:** 6.0 mm, single-lead, right-hand
- **Engagement:** when fully torqued, **0.8–1.2 turns of engagement** between cap and neck (this is what gives the airtight seal pressure)
- **Cap height to liner seat:** 9.5 mm minimum (so the cap liner contacts the rim sealing land at full torque, not before)

### Specifying GPI 63/2030 to the factory (copy-paste into PO email)

> *"All 250g jars must conform to GPI 63/2030 neck finish per ISO 9100. Required dimensions: T = 62.4 ± 0.4 mm, E = 60.2 ± 0.4 mm, I = 50.0 ± 0.5 mm, S = 66.0 ± 0.4 mm, neck height H = 12.5 ± 0.5 mm. Thread: 6.0 mm pitch, single-lead, right-hand, 2.5 turns nominal. Top sealing land: 1.5 mm flat ring, Ra ≤ 0.4 µm, flatness ≤ 0.1 mm, no parting line across rim. Parting line on body only. Sealing surface must be fire-polished or machined flat to support induction foil sealing (see §9 — Sealing & airtight integrity)."*

### Why GPI 63/2030 (not bespoke)

- Every cap manufacturer in India already tools for it — no custom mould cost
- Every induction-seal foil vendor sells 63 mm discs off the shelf
- Switching cap supplier later requires zero re-engineering
- The Western standard equivalent (SP-400) is dimensionally near-identical — exports stay easy

### Forbidden alternatives

- **Twist-off lug cap** (six-lug) — used on jam jars, looks dated for premium pickle; harder to source matching foil
- **Press-on plug fit** — leaks oil within weeks under transit vibration
- **Cork stopper** — not airtight, not food-safe for long shelf life, not what customers expect

## 2. Cap specification

### Base spec (v1 launch)

| Attribute | Spec |
|-----------|------|
| **Material** | **Tinplate** (food-grade) — preferred over aluminum for cost and finish compatibility |
| **Outer finish** | **Matte / satin warm champagne gold lacquer** (Pantone 872C or 874C reference — warm side of metallic gold). Stock finish, no printing needed. |
| **Inner coating** | **Food-grade lacquer rated for ACIDIC + OILY contents** (critical — uncoated metal will corrode and contaminate pickle) |
| **Closure type** | Twist-on (continuous thread) preferred over press-on lug cap |
| **Liner / gasket** | PVC-free, food-grade liner that holds an airtight seal |
| **Tamper evidence** | Either built-in tamper band on cap, OR confirm we'll add a separate PVC/PET shrink seal over the cap (we'll buy these separately if not part of cap) |
| **Diameter** | Matches 63mm jar mouth — confirm thread spec (likely 63/2030 or 63/400) |
| **Edge profile** | **Soft rounded top edge** (no sharp 90° corners) |

**Why gold over wood-look print:**
- Reinforces the brand name "Mother's **Gold** Spice" on every jar
- Cheaper (~₹2–4 vs ~₹4–8 per cap for printed wood) and more durable (no print to flake)
- Better surface for v2 foil-stamp logo (red, copper, or black foil on gold reads premium)
- Stock finish at every Indian cap manufacturer — easier to source, lower MOQ

**Specifically AVOID:**
- Mirror-chrome / shiny gold (looks cheap, holds fingerprints)
- Bright yellow gold (reads plastic / costume jewellery)
- Green-tinted gold (cool tone clashes with cream paper + spice-red label palette)

**Critical confirmation for the factory:** ask for **3–5 sample caps** in different gold finishes (between Pantone 871–876C range) so we can pick the one that pairs best with the cream bagasse paper label in person. The same gold can look different under daylight, supermarket fluorescents, and Instagram photography.

### Future-proofing for v2 branding (lock these in NOW, no extra cost)

We will keep the v1 cap plain (no logo), but in v2 we plan to add a small brand mark (likely **hot foil stamping** of an "MGS" monogram on the top centre). To make v2 a simple add-on rather than a full cap redesign:

| Constraint to enforce on v1 cap | Why |
|----------------------------------|-----|
| **Cap top surface must be flat or near-flat** (no heavy decorative embossing in the centre) | Hot foil stamping needs a clean flat surface |
| **Reserve a ~20mm circular "logo zone" at the top centre** | This area should remain unprinted (or only lightly printed with wood texture). Either show plain metal or a subtle wood pattern — but no busy print here. |
| **Confirm cap supplier offers hot foil stamping in-house** OR is willing to ship caps to a finishing vendor and re-certify the batch | Avoids a v2 supplier scramble. If they can't do foil in-house, get the name of a finishing vendor they trust. |
| **Avoid debossed company markings on cap top** (manufacturer's mould marks should be on the inside rim or skirt, not the top) | Ditto — keeps the top centre clean |

### Cap branding methods (reference table for v2/v3 decisions)

| Method | Look | Added cost/cap | MOQ | When to use |
|--------|------|---------------|-----|-------------|
| Hot foil stamping (recommended for v2) | Gold/copper/silver metallic monogram | ₹1–3 | 500–1,000 | Add a small "MGS" or mango-leaf motif on top centre for premium feel |
| Pad printing | Flat single-colour logo | ₹0.50–2 | 500 | Cheaper v2 alternative if budget is tight |
| Offset / lithographic | Full multi-colour design across the cap | ₹2–5 | 10,000+ | v3+: full brand wrap once we're at scale |
| Embossing + foil combo | Raised logo + gilded finish | ₹3–6 | 2,000+ | v3+: top-tier "gift jar" line |

Tooling cost for hot foil die: ~₹2,000–5,000 one-time per design (paid once, reused for every batch).

## 9. Sealing & airtight integrity

A pickle jar with mustard oil has to survive (a) air courier transit with shock and pressure changes, (b) 12 months on a hot kitchen shelf without oxidation of the oil, and (c) the customer's expectation that no oil leaks onto whatever else is in the bag. The cap alone doesn't do this. We use a **three-layer seal** — each layer covers a different failure mode.

> Visual mockup of how the layers stack lives on **[/label-preview](https://mothersgoldspice.com/label-preview)**, section 5.

### The three layers

| # | Layer | What it does | Failure mode it prevents |
|---|-------|--------------|---------------------------|
| 1 | **Cap liner / gasket** (inside the cap) | Compressible food-grade foam pressed between the cap threads and the glass rim when torqued | Primary mechanical seal — without this, even a "tight" cap leaks oil at the threads |
| 2 | **Induction foil seal** (heat-bonded to the glass rim under the cap) | A thin foil disc with heat-activated adhesive; bonded to the rim by induction heat after capping | Air-tight integrity, 12-month shelf life, tamper evidence (foil tears on first open) |
| 3 | **Tamper band** (joint-only PET ring, ~6 mm tall) | Thin printed band over the cap-to-neck seam only — *not* a full sleeve; cap surface stays visible | Visible tamper evidence for retail / quick-commerce shelves; preserves the premium gold-cap aesthetic |

### Layer-by-layer specifications

**Layer 1 — Cap liner**
- Material: **EPE foam** or **PE-coated pulp**, food-grade, certified for acidic + oily contents
- PVC-free (mandatory — PVC plasticizers migrate into oil)
- Thickness: 1.0–1.5 mm
- Pre-inserted into the cap by the cap manufacturer (no separate operation for us)
- Cost: bundled into the cap price (≈ ₹0.50–1.00 of the ₹2–4 cap cost)
- Confirm with factory: *"Liner must be PVC-free EPE/PE-coated, food-grade rated for mustard oil + acidic contents (pH ≥ 3.0)."*

**Layer 2 — Induction foil seal**
- A 63 mm foil disc with heat-activated polymer coating on the underside
- Material spec: **aluminium foil + PET + heat-seal layer** (standard "two-piece" liner)
- Sits between the cap liner and the glass rim before capping
- After capping, runs through an induction sealer — the inductor's magnetic field heats only the aluminium foil; the heat-seal layer melts and bonds the foil permanently to the glass rim
- Customer peels it off on first use; foil tears at the perforations leaving the rim clean
- Cost: ₹1–2 per jar (foil rolls — order from any induction-seal vendor on IndiaMART)
- Stock options: plain silver, plain gold, or **printed with brand mark / "SEALED FOR FRESHNESS"** (printed adds ~₹0.50)

**Layer 3 — Tamper band (joint-only, ~6 mm tall)**
- Material: **PET shrink film** (recyclable, food-safe)
- Height: **6 mm** — covers ONLY the cap-to-neck seam (NOT a full sleeve)
- Why thin: the gold cap finish + foil-stamped brand mark on the cap top are our premium signals — a full sleeve would hide them. A 6 mm band sits at the seam, gives tamper evidence, leaves the cap aesthetic intact.
- Position when applied: ~3 mm overlapping the bottom edge of the cap, ~3 mm down onto the neck (so the band spans the joint)
- Print: small repeating **"SEALED · Mother's Gold Spice · SMALL BATCH"** in cream on spice-red, or plain spice-red with a single perforation line
- Tear behaviour: a vertical perforation in the band tears the moment the cap is twisted — single-use, can't be re-applied
- Applied as a small loose tube over the capped jar, then shrunk with heat gun
- **Cost: ₹0.50–0.80 per jar** (≈ half the cost of a full sleeve thanks to less material + smaller print run)

**Why we chose joint-only over full sleeve:**
- Premium D2C positioning — gold cap + foil-stamped brand mark stay visible
- Cheaper per unit
- Still satisfies retail / quick-commerce shelf tamper-evidence requirements (Blinkit, Zepto, Big Basket, modern trade)
- Aligns with how premium European jam/pickle/honey brands handle tamper (small foil collar at neck, not full PVC sleeve)

**Total seal cost per jar: ₹2.50–4** on top of jar + cap + label.

### Equipment & process at pilot scale (500–1,000 jars)

| Step | What happens | Equipment | One-time cost |
|------|-------------|-----------|---------------|
| 1 | Wash + sterilize empty jars | Boiling water bath OR dry heat oven 120 °C × 30 min | ₹0 (use existing kitchen kit) |
| 2 | Hot-fill or cold-fill pickle + top up with mustard oil | Hand-fill with funnel for pilot | ₹0 |
| 3 | **Wipe rim absolutely clean** — critical, any oil residue here ruins the induction seal | Lint-free cloth + iso-alcohol wipe | ₹0 |
| 4 | Place foil disc on rim, place cap with liner on top, torque to spec | Hand-torque sealer (or hand for pilot, marked for consistency) | ₹2–5k |
| 5 | Run jar through induction sealer (1–3 seconds) — foil bonds to glass | **Handheld induction sealer** (300–500 jars/hr) | ₹15–25k |
| 6 | Slide tamper band over the cap-neck joint, apply heat to shrink | Heat gun (manual — pilot scale) or small shrink tunnel later | ₹2–3k (gun) |
| 7 | QC: visual inspection + invert 24h at 40 °C | Tray + warm room | ₹0 |

**Total pilot kit: ~₹20–35k one-time** for full in-house control.

**Alternative — co-pack at small commercial kitchen** that already owns this gear: pay ~₹3–8 per jar to fill + seal at small volumes. Lower CapEx, faster to launch, but no in-house control over hygiene + consistency. Acceptable for the very first pilot batch (Tier A — 500 jars). Move in-house at Tier B / launch.

### Quality checks per batch (mandatory)

Before any batch ships, pull **5 random jars** from the batch and run:

1. **Visual inspection** — foil seal intact, no air gaps; tamper band intact (no pre-tears) and centred on the cap-neck joint; cap torqued evenly
2. **Invert test** — invert each jar onto a clean paper towel for 24h at 40 °C (room temperature in Bangalore summer). *Zero oil staining = pass.* Any seepage = batch held back; root-cause the seal.
3. **Vacuum / pop test** — press the centre of the foil seal through the cap (where possible to feel through plastic caps; with tinplate, do this after opening the sample). Should feel firm, not click in.
4. **Destruct sample** — open 1 of the 5, smell the oil. Should smell of fresh mustard oil, no rancid notes. Taste-test the pickle. Document any off-notes.

Keep a one-page log per batch (Date, Batch No., Jars filled, Jars passed QC, Jars rejected, Notes). Required for FSSAI traceability and very useful when scaling.

### What we explicitly avoid

| Option | Why we say no |
|--------|----------------|
| Wax-dipped cap (vintage look) | Beautiful but slow, unscalable, melts above 40 °C — common in Indian summers |
| Vacuum seal via hot-fill | Mango pickle is filled cold/warm (preserves flavour); hot-fill at 85 °C kills the recipe's character |
| Cling-film under the cap (home practice) | Not food-grade for oil contact, not airtight, doesn't tamper-evident |
| Cap alone with no foil + no sleeve | This is what the cheap end of the pickle market does. It leaks in transit, oxidises in 4–6 months on shelf, and offers no tamper evidence. Below our brand bar. |
